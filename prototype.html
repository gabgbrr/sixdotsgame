Mechanics and Blueprints behind Six Dots

Mechanics

The game is designed to bridge the accessibility gap by allowing visually impaired and sighted children to engage in a shared arithmetic challenge. At its core, the game revolves around using math operations to interact with specific point values on a custom board. Players rely on tactile cues, such as the unique indents at the top of each card, to ensure they are holding the card correctly before reading the Braille value positioned just below the printed number. This multi-sensory design ensures that every player can identify their hand independently and participate in the calculations without needing sighted assistance.


Here is how you play the game:

  1. Preparation: Distribute the three types of cards—Point cards, Math cards Set 1, and Math cards Set 2—which are easily distinguishable by their specific indents.
  2. Identifying Targets: Locate the Point cards on the custom board; these represent the target values that players need to reach or "defuse".
  3. Calclation Phase:U se the Math cards in your hand to perform arithmetic operations aimed at matching the value of a Point card.
  4. Verification: Confirm card values through the Braille labels created with a braille slate tool, or use the optional QR-code audio feedback for digital confirmation.
  5. Scoring: Successfully "defuse" the point cards by reaching the correct sum or result to earn points for your team or yourself.
  6. Collaborative Play: Throughout the game, sighted and visually impaired players can communicate and strategize together, as the high-contrast colors and tactile markings make the game state clear to everyone.
